My Essays

Reflections on my application writing process

Here are some reflections on essays from my own application. I share these to illustrate my approach, not as templates to copy.

My personal statement explored how working at at a young age taught me empathy and the passion to help others. The challenge was being vulnerable and using descriptive detail whilst being concise.

What worked: Starting with a metaphor, then expanding to show personal growth without being overly dramatic. I focused on authentic details rather than trying to sound impressive. A bookending also kept the story memorable.

Advice: Your most meaningful stories often come from everyday moments, not extraordinary events.

This essay explained why I wanted to study biology through the lens of an issue faced by many low-income students.

What worked: Connecting academic interests to personal experiences. I showed genuine curiosity rather than repeating what I thought admissions wanted to hear.

Advice: Be specific about why you're drawn to a field. "I've always been interested in biology" is less compelling than describing the moment that sparked your curiosity.

I wrote about a project that initially struggled but eventually succeeded through consistent effort and passion.

What worked: Showing vulnerability by admitting the project's early failures, then demonstrating growth through problem-solving. This felt more authentic than a "success story."

Advice: Don't be afraid to show challenges or setbacks—they often reveal more about your character than easy successes.
